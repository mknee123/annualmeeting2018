Welcome to the Allegheny Conference’s 2018 Annual Meeting Report

By Bill Demchak & Stefani Pashman

For nearly 75 years, the Pittsburgh region has collaborated on the work to improve this place we call home.

If everyone believes the press clippings we’ve received over the past year, we really don’t have anything to worry about as a region anymore.

We’ve made it.

We’re a global darling.

What more do we need to do for the future?

In reality, the answer is “a lot.” We have a lot to be concerned about and a lot more we need to do in our region.

Here’s what we need to realize: We can’t believe the press clippings, the impressions, nor the instant satisfaction on social media of the latest headline or award or list.

It’s great PR for the region – and we should certainly feel proud and permitted to tout Pittsburgh as one of the best in the world – but by all means we cannot be complacent and call ourselves a success just yet.

This may seem contradictory. How can we be proud of a place that has a lot of work to do? The answer is because not everyone or every place in Pittsburgh has achieved a better life. We have a standard of excellence here in the region, and everyone has a right to experience it. Even with tangible success in some places and in some sectors, we need to reach further because somewhere in the 10-counties of our region we haven’t done enough.

This past year was the first year of our 2018-19 Agenda: Creating a Next Generation Economy for All. We laid the foundation for improving economic inclusion, for marketing our region as a place for top talent and innovative companies to thrive, and for measuring the progress of our region. We helped a number of critical pieces of legislation progress and pass through the state legislature that will improve business for manufacturers and remove barriers for parts of our population that haven’t had the opportunity for economic mobility.

We set the scene for 2019 and beyond. In the coming months, we aim to move swiftly on competitiveness issues we’ve long seen as a detriment to the Pittsburgh region, like tax reform, infrastructure and sustainability. We’re aiming to connect the region’s next generation of leaders to engage with us on an initiative to prepare the region for when they assume leadership. Through the leadership of the region, we’re fortunate to have a path to follow to improve. Let’s do the same for tomorrow’s leadership.

One thing we benefit from in Pittsburgh is a high standard of work ethic, collaboration and empathy to move ahead together. We will undoubtedly witness more rankings and headlines, but if we keep our standard of excellence in mind we will never be complacent, and we’ll always seek ways to work together to meet and exceed our standards.

Sincerely,
