Growing the Economy

We’re focusing economic development strategies to grow our economy through business attraction and retention across five strategic sectors, including a focus on innovation and energy-related manufacturing.

In 2018, this strategy was chaired by Laura Karet, Vice Chair of the Allegheny Conference and CEO, Giant Eagle, Inc.

Innovation Economic Development

The Pittsburgh Regional Alliance – the Allegheny Conference’s economic development marketing affiliate – attracted a signature innovation-related project to the region.

The Allegheny Conference participated in the regional team that formulated and submitted Pittsburgh’s Amazon HQ2 proposal. The region was named as a Top 20 finalist for the project, which has significantly raised the profile of Pittsburgh for additional technology investment and media attention.

Energy-Related Manufacturing

The Shell Pennsylvania Chemicals petrochemical facility is under construction and growing by the day. An economic development win from 2016, the facility now hosts 2,950 construction workers, and key pieces of equipment vital to the operation of the ethane cracker arrive routinely.

The cracker is paying dividends, and it’s not even operating yet. Six new supply chain projects related to Shell’s facility are underway in the Pittsburgh region.

The passage of PA House Bill 1419, or “Clean Slate” legislation, “Clean Slate” is an important step in addressing the Pittsburgh region’s workforce challenge while expanding economic inclusion to all of our communities. The bill provides for automatic sealing of criminal records for certain individuals with misdemeanor convictions, allowing potential employers to look past minor mistakes and focus on the qualifications of the individual.

A key piece of legislation – HB163 – passed the house and is ready to move in the Pennsylvania senate. This bill would reform the policy of suspending driver’s licenses for minor offenses, which serves as a barrier to employment and reduction in quality of life. This issue has bipartisan support and would improve the economic future of many.

In 2018, this strategy was chaired by Grant Oliphant, President, The Heinz Endowments.
